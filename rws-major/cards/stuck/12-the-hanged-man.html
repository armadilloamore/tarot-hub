Internal Block

You may be stuck because you’re trying to force forward movement in a situation that actually requires surrender, patience, or a shift in perspective. Part of you may fear stopping, slowing down, or releasing control — especially if your worth feels tied to productivity.

Psychological Frame

This individuation stage teaches the value of suspension — pausing long enough to see from a new angle. The Hanged Man represents ego loosening rather than failure. Resistance appears when you equate not acting with losing.

Where You May Be Resisting

  • Impatience with a slow or unclear process
  • Forcing solutions instead of allowing clarity
  • Fear of stillness or pausing
  • Discomfort with uncertainty or non-action

Coaching Question

“What happens if I allow this situation to unfold instead of trying to control it?”

Practice

  • Identify one area where you can release control today.
  • Pause before acting — even for 10 seconds — and breathe.
  • Ask yourself: “What new perspective is this delay trying to reveal?”

Core Insight

“Growth often requires suspension, not speed.”
